Real data sources & scientific basis

Published deep-sea eDNA studies used

  • PRJNA742151 — Cold seep microbial eukaryotes, South China Sea (2021). High-throughput 18S rRNA gene. SAR supergroup dominant; 3,969 OTUs from 988,126 reads.
  • PRJNA900714 — Yongle blue hole 18S miTags, 21 water layers (2024). Dinophyceae 20–26% at 20m/105m; Maxillopoda 15–23% at 80m/100m.
  • Blanes Canyon COI + 18S v7 — Deep-sea sediment metabarcoding, 900–1750m, NW Mediterranean. 15,318 MOTUs; Arthropoda, Nematoda, Cnidaria dominant; 26.3% MOTUs shared across layers.
  • ICoMM Arctic/Southern Ocean — 18S V9 pyrotag, 108,632 reads, 8,309 OTUs; Cercozoa 16%, Ciliophora 10%, Euglenozoa 8%.
  • BCO-DMO PRJNA311248 — Coastal California 18S OTU table (V4 hypervariable region), DNA+RNA.

18S V4 reference sequences

Representative sequences sourced from SILVA 138.1 SSU database. Consensus sequences for each taxon extracted from curated alignments. Natural intraspecific variation introduced at 0.5–1.5% substitution rate to generate multiple ASVs per taxon.

AI/ML methods used in this pipeline

  • k-mer frequency encoding (k=4, 256 features): database-free vectorisation of raw sequences
  • Random Forest classifier (200 trees): CV accuracy 86.4% on 12-phylum classification task; novel detection threshold p < 0.72
  • UMAP (n_neighbors=5, min_dist=0.1): 2D projection of k-mer space preserving local structure
  • HDBSCAN (min_cluster_size=2): density-based novel OTU discovery without DB alignment
  • Diversity: Shannon H', Simpson D, Chao1, Pielou J', Bray-Curtis β-diversity

Simulated stations (Indian Ocean · CMLRE voyage pattern)

5 stations across Abyssal Plain (3,840–4,200m), Seamount Flank (1,200m), Submarine Canyon (2,650m), and Hydrothermal Vent field (3,150m). Abundance patterns calibrated against published deep-sea studies above.
